About Me

Research & Develop

I am a developer with a focus on artificial intelligence and machine learning applications. My work aims to bridge theoretical concepts with practical implementations to solve real-world problems.

Currently, I am working as a Research Assistant and Teaching Assistant at the University of Toledo, focusing on AI and ML research in hardware security. My research interests include developing advanced algorithms for hardware Trojan detection, creating synthetic datasets using generative models, and building intelligent agents for automated security analysis.

Prior to my research role, I worked as a Salesforce Developer for two years, where I designed and implemented custom CRM solutions for enterprise clients. This experience has given me a strong foundation in software development, database management, and client-focused problem solving.

My goal is to contribute to the advancement of AI-driven security solutions, making technology safer and more reliable through innovative research and development.
